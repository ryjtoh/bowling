The slope formula y = mx + b is a crucial concept to grasp in algebra. It tells you a straight (linear) line's behavior and is a formula for finding points on that line. Here's a rundown of the variables in the formula: ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ x : tells the horizontal position on the coordinate system (left/right) y : tells the vertical position on the coordinate system (up/down) m : the slope, which tell you how steep the line is if you can imagine the positive y direction to be pointing toward the sky! b : the y-intercept, where the straight line hit the y-axis (vertical axis) ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ Now that you know the terminology of the slope formula, lets visualize it using this interactive coordinate system. - Use the slope slider to control the steepness of the line (Hint: Increasing slope makes a more steep line!) - Use the y-intercept slider to control where the line hits the y-axis (Hint: Increasing y-intercept raises the line!) ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ ━ Instructions: There are going to be 2 golf holes on the coordinate system that fit a particular linear line. 2 golf balls will spawn from the "middle" of the line, so plan your line carefully!
